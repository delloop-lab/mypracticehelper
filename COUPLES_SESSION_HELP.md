# Booking sessions with two clients

A short guide for adding a second client (a partner) to an appointment so the same session shows up in both clients' records.

## What's new

You can now add a second person to a single appointment. This is useful when you see a couple together: instead of creating two separate appointments, you create one appointment with both clients on it.

If you only have one client in the appointment, nothing changes. Everything works exactly as before.

## When to use it

Use a two-client appointment when both people will attend the same session at the same time, for example:

- Couples therapy
- Two family members in the same session
- Any joint session where the notes and recording belong to both clients

If the two people are seen separately (different times), keep using two separate single-client appointments.

## How to add a second client to an appointment

When creating a new appointment, or when editing an existing one:

1. Pick the first client like you always do (this is the primary client).
2. Click "Add second participant" and choose the second client from the list.
3. Save the appointment.

That's it. The appointment now belongs to both clients.

You can only add a second client who already exists in your client list. If they're not there yet, add them as a client first, then come back and attach them to the appointment.

## How to recognise a two-client session at a glance

You'll see clear signals so you're never in doubt:

- The appointment in your calendar/list shows both names: "Anna + John".
- A small "2 participants" badge appears on the appointment.
- When you open the appointment, the title clearly says "Couples session: Anna + John".
- Inside the session, a banner reminds you: "Shared session with {partner}. Notes and recordings are visible to both clients."
- On a single client's Sessions list, the row shows "with {partner}" so you know who else was there.

## What gets shared between the two clients

For a two-client appointment, the following are shared between both clients:

- The appointment itself (date, time, type, venue, duration).
- Session notes you write.
- Audio recordings and transcripts.
- Anything attached to the session.

Open the session from either client's record and you'll see the same information. Anything you change in one place updates for both at the same time.

## What is NOT shared between the two clients

The following stay private to each client (as they always have):

- Each client's profile, contact details, intake forms, documents.
- Each client's other appointments.
- Each client's personal session history (only the joint sessions appear in both records, never the solo ones).

## Editing a two-client appointment

When you edit a two-client appointment:

- Whatever you change applies to both clients automatically (it's a single appointment, not a copy).
- The app shows a small note: "Changes will apply to both Anna and John."
- You can also remove the second client at any time. The app will warn: "This will remove the session from John's records. Anna will keep the session." After saving, the appointment goes back to being a normal one-client session under the primary client.

## Deleting a two-client appointment

When you delete a two-client appointment:

- The confirmation will say: "This will remove the session from both Anna and John's records."
- Confirming deletes the single shared appointment, removing it from both clients' Sessions tabs at once.

## Fees and payment

A two-client appointment has one fee and one payment, not two. This is intentional: it's a single session attended by two people.

- The fee/payment is tracked under the primary client (the first one you selected).
- Reminders for unpaid sessions appear under the primary client only, so you don't get reminded twice for the same session.
- Revenue isn't double-counted on the Payments page.

If you ever need to charge each person separately, create two separate single-client appointments instead.

## Single-client sessions are unchanged

If an appointment has only one client, nothing about it has changed:

- It looks the same.
- It only appears in that one client's record.
- Notes, recordings, fees, payments, reminders all behave exactly as they did before.

## Quick FAQ

- Q: I added a second client by mistake. What do I do?
  - Edit the appointment and clear the second participant, then save. The session goes back to the primary client only.

- Q: Can I add three or more people?
  - Not yet. Two participants only.

- Q: Will the second client see the first client's name in their own profile?
  - Only on the joint session row (it shows "with {partner}"). Their profile, contact details, and other sessions remain private.

- Q: I started taking notes from one client's view. Will the partner's view see them?
  - Yes — the notes belong to the session, not to a single client. Both views show the same notes and recording.

- Q: Does this affect any existing appointments?
  - No. Existing appointments remain exactly as they were. The second-participant feature only kicks in for appointments where you explicitly add one.

If anything is unclear, contact your developer/support and they can walk through it with you.
